Crop Qualities Overview

Tomatoes and bell peppers are perishable crops that require careful handling to maintain quality. Key qualities considered during sorting include:
• Condition – Detecting visible damage such as bruises, blemishes, and discoloration.
• Color – Classifying undamaged crops as green or red to meet buyer packaging and pricing requirements.
• Size – Categorizing undamaged crops into small, medium, or large for consistent presentation and market standards.

Importance of Sorting

Proper sorting ensures high-quality produce reaches the market, reducing post-harvest losses and payment deductions due to damaged or misclassified crops. Sorting by damage, color, and size:
  • Improves buyer confidence and reduces rejected batches.
  • Helps vendors price accurately by avoiding mixed sizes or colors in the same batch.
  • Supports faster operations, minimizing labor and time spent on manual inspection.
  • Prevents loss of income by removing damaged produce before packaging.
